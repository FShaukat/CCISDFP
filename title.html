Title Programs

Title Programs are federally funded initiatives under the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) that promote equity and excellence in schools. CCISD manages several Title Programs to support student achievement and ensure access to educational opportunities for all.

Programs We Manage

Title I, Part A – Improving Basic Programs

Provides supplemental academic support to campuses with high percentages of students from low-income families. Funds target reading, math, and other core academic areas to improve overall student achievement.

  • Evidence-based interventions and curriculum support (ESSA Sec. 1114)
  • Extended learning opportunities, including tutoring and after-school programs
  • Professional development for instructional staff on effective teaching practices
  • Parent and family engagement activities and school–parent compacts

Title II, Part A – Supporting Effective Instruction

Aims to improve teacher and principal effectiveness by investing in professional development, mentorship, and leadership capacity.

  • Targeted PD aligned with Texas Teacher Evaluation and Support System (T-TESS)
  • Induction and coaching programs for new and developing educators
  • Support for teacher recruitment, certification pathways, and retention strategies

Statutory Authority: ESSA Title II, Part A (Sections 2101–2103)

Title III, Part A – English Learners & Immigrant Students

Supports the academic achievement of English learners (ELs) and eligible immigrant students. Funds must supplement—not supplant—core services.

  • Enhanced language acquisition programs and dual language support
  • Professional development for ESL and general education teachers
  • Parent outreach and communication in families’ native languages

Statutory Authority: ESSA Title III, Part A (Sections 3101–3203)

Title IV, Part A – Student Support & Academic Enrichment (SSAE)

Promotes a well-rounded education by supporting enrichment programs, safe learning environments, and technology integration.

  • STEAM programming, music, and arts education
  • Behavioral and mental health supports including SEL initiatives
  • Access to high-quality digital tools and instructional technology

Statutory Authority: ESSA Title IV, Part A (Section 4101 et seq.)

Title IX, Part A – McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act

Guarantees educational access and support for students experiencing homelessness, ensuring they have equal opportunities to succeed in school.

  • Immediate enrollment even without typically required documents
  • Transportation to the school of origin
  • Access to school supplies, tutoring, and liaison support

Statutory Authority: McKinney-Vento Act (42 U.S.C. § 11431 et seq.)

State Compensatory Education (SCE) & Bilingual/ESL Programs

SCE provides supplemental instruction for students identified as at-risk of dropping out under Texas Education Code §29.081. Bilingual/ESL programs ensure students identified as English learners have access to appropriate language instruction aligned with Chapter 89 of the Texas Administrative Code.

  • Pull-out and push-in bilingual support models
  • Early exit and dual language program implementation
  • Progress monitoring and TELPAS support

District Impact & Eligibility

Each Title Program supports campuses based on specific statutory eligibility requirements and student needs:

  • Title I, Part A: Allocations are based on campus-level low-income data. CCISD uses a schoolwide model to deliver services to all students on eligible campuses (ESSA Sec. 1114).
  • Title II, Part A: Districtwide allocation used for educator support, including PD, leadership pipelines, and equity strategies. Fund use must align with a documented needs assessment (ESSA Sec. 2102).
  • Title III, Part A: Eligibility is based on the number of English learners and immigrant students enrolled. Funds supplement core ESL services (ESSA Sec. 3115).
  • Title IV, Part A: Formula-based allocation for academic enrichment, student health/safety, and digital learning. Districts must conduct a needs assessment if receiving >$30,000 (ESSA Sec. 4106).
  • Title IX, Part A: Services provided to students identified as homeless under McKinney-Vento Act protections. Eligibility is determined by living situation, not income.

About the Federal Programs Department

The Federal Programs Department at CCISD ensures that all students—especially those with the greatest needs—have access to high-quality educational opportunities. We manage federal and state-funded programs that promote academic equity, ensure compliance, and expand access to strategic funding across the district.

We serve as a bridge between schools, departments, and funding agencies—helping campuses navigate complex requirements while maximizing the impact of federal resources.

Our Mission

To ensure equitable access to quality education by managing federal resources with integrity, strengthening district capacity, and building sustainable systems of support through strategic funding and compliance services.

What We Do

Program Leadership

We oversee federal and state entitlement programs that provide critical support for student success, including:

  • Title I, Part A – Improving Basic Programs
  • Title III, Part A – Language Instruction for English Learners & Immigrant Students
  • Title IV, Part A – Enrichment & Well-Rounded Education
  • Title IX, Part A – McKinney-Vento Homeless Support
  • State Compensatory Education & Bilingual/ESL programs

We provide campus and district leaders with guidance on program implementation, eligibility, and allowable use of funds.

Grants

We support schools and departments in identifying, applying for, and managing grants from TEA, federal agencies, and private foundations. Our support includes:

  • Locating grant opportunities aligned with district goals
  • Reviewing proposals for alignment and compliance
  • Facilitating internal approvals and documentation
  • Monitoring timelines, reporting, and budgetary compliance

Compliance & Documentation

We develop tools, calendars, and resources to support federal program compliance. Our work helps ensure CCISD remains audit-ready and aligned with all state and federal requirements. Resources include:

  • Annual compliance calendars
  • Federal Programs Handbook
  • MOE, Time & Effort, and Comparability templates
  • eCrate submission guidance for Title I documentation

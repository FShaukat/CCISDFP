Grants Portal

The CCISD Grants Office is committed to securing funding that supports innovation, equity, and student success across the district. The Grants Portal is your central hub for information, tools, and support related to competitive and discretionary grant opportunities across the district. Whether you're a teacher seeking classroom resources, a campus leader exploring funding for innovative programs, or a department pursuing district-wide initiatives, the portal connects you to the resources you need to effectively collaborate on planning, applying, and managing grants effectively.

We support CCISD’s mission by ensuring transparency, sustainability, and strategic alignment in all grant-related efforts.

We believe great ideas live in every corner of the district. This portal is here to help you turn those ideas into impact — no experience required. If you have a goal or vision for your students, we want to hear about it!

Start Your Grant Application

If you're ready to pursue an external competitive grant, please begin by submitting the Intent to Apply form. This helps the Grants Department coordinate submissions, ensure compliance, and provide support with timelines, approvals, and proposal development.

Submitting this form is the first step in partnering with the district for grant success. Internal funding opportunities such as CCEF are not included in this process.

What We Do

  • Research and evaluate funding opportunities
  • Draft proposals with campus and department input
  • Prepare budgets aligned with CCISD policies and grantor rules
  • Manage internal approvals and submission processes
  • Ensure compliance, reporting, and sustainability

Types of Grants We Pursue

TEA Grants: Support instructional innovation, educator pipelines, and academic recovery. Submitted via eGrants or GEMS.

Office of the Governor Grants: Fund safety, mental health, and emergency preparedness. Often require matching and monthly reporting.

Federal Grants: Large-scale, high-dollar awards for STEM, infrastructure, equity. Governed by 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Grant Guidance).

Foundation & Private Grants: Support enrichment, innovation, and PD. Typically simpler but rarely include indirect cost recovery.

How Can We Support You?

  • Submit a Grant Wishlist: Have a dream project but no funding yet? Let us know so we can look out for matching grant opportunities.
  • Collaborate on a Grant: Found a grant and want help applying or partnering? We’re happy to support or co-apply with you.
  • Request Grant Support: Already writing a grant? We can help review, edit, or meet with you to strengthen your proposal.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a grant wishlist?
The wishlist allows staff to share ideas that need funding. The Grants Office will track potential matches and notify you when a fitting opportunity becomes available.
Can I get help editing or reviewing my grant application?
Yes! Use the Request Support form to request proofreading, editing, or a 1:1 consultation. We’re here to help every step of the way.
Who should use the Collaborate form?
If you’ve found a grant and want to apply with the district’s support—or want the Grants Office to serve as a co-applicant—this form helps us partner with you effectively.

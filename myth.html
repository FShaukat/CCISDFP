Grant Myths

Thinking about applying for a grant but not sure what’s true? Let’s clear the air! Below we debunk some of the most common myths that discourage educators and staff from applying — and show you how CCISD is here to help.

Anyone at CCISD can submit a strong grant proposal — with support from the Grants Office every step of the way.
Myth 1: “Grants are only for big, district-wide projects.”

Many grants are designed for classrooms, grade levels, or individual teacher initiatives. Even small ideas can be “projectified” into strong proposals — like resources for a science lab, supplies for reading centers, or enrichment activities.

Myth 2: “I need lots of grant-writing experience to apply.”

You don’t need to be an expert! The Grants Office can guide you through every step: from intent to apply, project design, budgeting, writing, and submission. We also have templates and tips to help you get started.

Myth 3: “Grant applications take too much time.”

Some grants are complex, but many are short applications tailored for classroom projects or programs. With good planning and the Grant Toolkit, most proposals can be completed efficiently — especially with help from the Grants Office.

Myth 4: “You have to match funds to apply for a grant.”

Not all grants require matching funds. In fact, many grants for classrooms, enrichment, or educator development have no match requirement. When a match is required, the Grants Office will help you understand options and approvals.

Myth 5: “Grants are only for new, innovative ideas.”

While some grants reward innovation, many fund expansions or improvements of what you’re already doing — like extending a successful after-school program or adding materials to strengthen an existing unit.

Myth 6: “You can’t ask for things like books or supplies.”

Actually, many grants fund tangible classroom resources, as long as you connect them to a defined project and clear outcomes. For example: “Literacy kits to support struggling readers” or “STEM materials to enhance inquiry labs.”

Myth 7: “If I submit an application, I’m on my own.”

At CCISD, you’re never on your own. We have a Grants Manager dedicated to helping with every phase of the process — and who can even manage the writing, approvals, and submission with you.

Myth 8: “If I don’t win the first time, I shouldn’t bother.”

Not true! Many successful applicants didn’t get funded their first try. Every proposal is a learning opportunity, and often funders provide feedback so you can strengthen your next application.

Still unsure? Contact the CCISD Grants Office for friendly guidance — we’re here to help you succeed.
